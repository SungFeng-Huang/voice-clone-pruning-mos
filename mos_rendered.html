Intructions

Please read the instructions carefully before answering the questions.

There are 2 sections below, each section with 9 testing audio clips and 2 reference recordings correspond to a target speaker.
That is, there would be 9+2 audio clips in each of the 2 sections, where the total duration of the recordings in this HIT would be in 0.5 to 1.5 minutes.

For each testing audio clip (real or generated speech), there are 3 questions (speaker similarity, accent similarity and naturalness) to rate.
Limited to the MTurk UI, each question occupy the page and would make the comparison difficult if the reference recordings only appear once in the beginning of the section.
Therefore, for easier comparison, we would repeatedly put the test and reference recordings in each question.
If you've listened to the reference recordings in the beginning of each section, or listened to the test recording before its 3 questions, it is not required to listen to them again in each question. It's opt to you to listen to each recording only once at the beginning or multiple times for better judgements.

Limited to the MTurk UI, each question occupy the page and making the HIT seemed to be very long.
However, bare in mind that you should rate according to your 1st impression and don't take too much time thinking. So it won't take too much time to finish it.
The total duration of the audio clips is in 0.5 to 1.5 minutes, and the rest of the time is for you to make your best judgements.

The following answering instructions would also be placed in each question as a reminder. It is not required to read them again in each question.

Speaker Similarity

There are 2 reference recordings of the target speaker and 1 testing audio sample in each question:

  • Please listen carefully to each sample. You must listen to both the reference and the test recordings before selecting your answer.
  • Judge how similar is the testing audio's voice comparing to the target speaker.
  • If the recording sounds very differently with different biological sex, select "Bad - Completely different voice".
  • If the recording sounds very differently while with the same biological sex, select "Poor - Mostly dissimilar voice".
  • Note that, an audio sample can be heavily-accented and difficult to understand while being "Excellent - Completely the same voice". Please use your best judgements.

Accent Similarity

There are 2 reference recordings of the target speaker and 1 testing audio sample in each question:

  • Please listen carefully to each sample. You must listen to both the reference and the test recordings before selecting your answer.
  • Judge how similar is the testing audio's accent comparing to the target speaker.
  • Note that, an audio sample can sound like different speaker and be difficult to understand while being "Excellent - Completely the same accent". Please use your best judgements.

Naturalness

  • You must listen to the entire audio clip before rating.
  • You may play each clip a maximum of three times.
  • You should take fluency, audio quality, and style consistency across sentences into account.
  • Note that, an audio clip can be heavily-accented and difficult to understand while being "Excellent - Completely natural speech".
  • Note that, an audio clip can be not intelligible and contains missing words while being "Excellent - Completely natural speech". Please use your best jedgements.
  • For instance, the reference recordings should be considered "Excellent - Completely natural speech", even though they might have a heavy accent.
